Cathy is a 67-year-old woman in strong metabolic and hematologic health — her March 2026 CBC is entirely clean, with all 17 markers normal and no flags. However, her September 2025 DEXA scan reveals a clinically significant bone health problem: WHO-confirmed osteoporosis at the left femoral neck (T-score −2.7), with the right femoral neck approaching the threshold (−2.3) and low bone mass at the spine (−1.4). The referring physician has recommended pharmacologic intervention and this is now Cathy's most urgent medical priority.

Her genetic profile adds important context. The MTHFR C677T variant impairs the methylation cycle, reducing calcium absorption efficiency and increasing homocysteine — both of which accelerate bone loss. The COMT homozygous slow variant affects estrogen clearance; post-menopausal estrogen decline is the primary driver of bone loss in women, and impaired estrogen metabolism may have worsened her trajectory. Her CFH variant raises macular degeneration risk. Her ADD1 variant increases salt-sensitive blood pressure risk. The standout positive is her homozygous ApoE2 genotype — significantly protective against Alzheimer's disease.

The supplement protocol has been updated to include calcium and additional Vitamin D specifically for bone health, on top of the already-indicated MTHFR, eye, and cardiovascular stack.
Osteoporosis diagnosis confirmed by WHO criteria. Pharmacologic intervention (bisphosphonates or equivalent) should be discussed with Dr. Gulati immediately. DEXA follow-up recommended September 2027.

Genetic-bone connection
Cathy's bone loss likely has both hormonal and genetic drivers. Post-menopausal estrogen decline is the primary cause of accelerated bone loss in women — her COMT homozygous slow variant affects estrogen clearance, which may have contributed to an atypical estrogen metabolism pattern through the transition. Her MTHFR C677T variant elevates homocysteine, which is independently associated with increased fracture risk and impaired bone collagen cross-linking. These two genetic factors are likely amplifying what would otherwise be typical post-menopausal bone loss. Calcium absorption (aided by Vitamin D + K2) and methylation support (5-MTHF) both directly address bone metabolism.

Genetic mutation analysis
Source: GenVue Discovery variant report. Heterozygous = one copy; Homozygous = two copies. All variants are research/educational — not diagnostic.
MTHFR C677T — heterozygous
rs1801133  ·  Chromosome 1  ·  Methylation / folate metabolism
High priority
The MTHFR enzyme converts dietary folate into active 5-methyltetrahydrofolate (5-MTHF) — the usable form that powers the methylation cycle. This cycle controls DNA repair, neurotransmitter production, homocysteine clearance, and detoxification. One copy of the thermolabile C677T variant reduces enzyme efficiency by ~35%. Standard folic acid (in fortified foods and cheap vitamins) cannot be processed and can block active folate receptors. Elevated homocysteine — the downstream result — is associated with cardiovascular disease and, critically, impaired bone collagen cross-linking that accelerates bone loss. Her MCV of 91 is reassuring (no acute anemia), but functional methylation deficit can exist without CBC changes — only a homocysteine blood test confirms impact.
Elevated homocysteine Cardiovascular risk Bone collagen impairment Impaired detoxification Folic acid intolerance Mood dysregulation
Action: Start methylfolate (5-MTHF) + methyl-B12 + riboflavin (Thorne Methyl-Guard Plus). Avoid all synthetic folic acid. Test homocysteine urgently — especially relevant to bone health.
COMT Val158Met — homozygous (AA)
rs4680  ·  Chromosome 22  ·  Catecholamine + estrogen clearance
High priority
COMT (catechol-O-methyltransferase) breaks down dopamine, adrenaline, and critically — estrogen metabolites. Two copies of the slow variant means these compounds are cleared significantly more slowly than average. Post-menopausal bone loss is primarily driven by estrogen decline; impaired estrogen clearance may have created an atypical hormonal trajectory through menopause that contributed to her bone loss pattern. On the neurological side, this is the "worrier" genotype — heightened emotional memory, stronger stress response, and caffeine sensitivity. COMT requires magnesium as a cofactor and benefits directly from the methylation support in the MTHFR protocol.
Slow estrogen clearance Stress + anxiety sensitivity Caffeine sensitivity Dopamine accumulation Bone loss amplifier
Action: Evening magnesium glycinate (COMT cofactor). Moderate caffeine. Stress management is gene-relevant, not just lifestyle advice.
CFH His402 — heterozygous
rs1061170  ·  Chromosome 1  ·  Complement system / retinal inflammation
Moderate-High
Complement Factor H regulates the immune system's complement cascade in the retina. This variant disrupts that regulation, allowing chronic low-grade inflammation to accumulate beneath the macula — responsible for sharp central vision. This is one of the strongest known genetic risk factors for age-related macular degeneration (AMD). AMD is silent in early stages; regular dilated eye exams are the only way to catch it before vision is affected. The AREDS2 supplement formula was specifically developed and clinically validated for patients with this exact genetic risk profile.
Age-related macular degeneration Basal laminar drusen Retinal inflammation
Action: Annual dilated eye exam — urgent. Start Vision Defender AREDS2 formula immediately.
ADD1 Gly460Trp — heterozygous
rs4961  ·  Chromosome 4  ·  Salt-sensitive blood pressure
Watch
Alpha-adducin is involved in renal sodium reabsorption. This variant increases sodium retention, making Cathy's blood pressure more responsive to dietary salt intake than average — the same sodium load will raise her BP more than it would for most people. This doesn't mean she has hypertension, but it means dietary sodium is a more important cardiovascular lever for her than for the general population. Potassium intake (which physiologically counteracts sodium) and magnesium both directly support BP regulation for this genotype.
Salt-sensitive hypertension risk Increased sodium retention
Action: Monitor dietary sodium. Potassium-rich diet (sweet potato, avocado, greens). Magnesium supports BP.
AMPD1 — heterozygous carrier
rs17602729  ·  Chromosome 1  ·  Muscle energy metabolism
Low-Moderate
Myoadenylate deaminase is involved in ATP energy recycling within muscle cells during exercise. As a heterozygous carrier, Cathy is unlikely to manifest full AMPD1 deficiency, but may experience faster exercise-induced muscle fatigue and slower recovery compared to age-matched peers. This is relevant for exercise planning — particularly resistance training, which is the most effective intervention for osteoporosis. Lower intensity with adequate rest between sessions is appropriate. CoQ10 and magnesium both support the energy metabolism pathway.
Exercise fatigue sensitivity Slower muscle recovery
Action: Moderate-intensity resistance training for bone health. Allow full recovery. CoQ10 + magnesium support energy metabolism.
APOE rs7412 — homozygous ApoE2 (TT)
rs7412  ·  Chromosome 19  ·  Lipid metabolism / neurodegeneration
Protective
ApoE2 is the rarest and most protective APOE variant. Homozygous ApoE2 is associated with significantly reduced risk of Alzheimer's disease compared to the common ApoE3 or high-risk ApoE4 variants — this is genuinely good news. One monitoring consideration: homozygous ApoE2 can occasionally be associated with familial type 3 hyperlipoproteinemia (elevated triglycerides and cholesterol). A fasting lipid panel will confirm whether this is a factor — it's not on the current CBC panel.
Reduced Alzheimer's risk Monitor lipid panel Type 3 hyperlipoproteinemia watch
Action: Annual fasting lipid panel. No urgent concern — this is the standout positive genetic finding.
